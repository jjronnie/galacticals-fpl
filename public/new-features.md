# New Profile Stats Features

## 1. Form Guide / Rolling Average
Average points over the last 5 gameweeks vs season average. Color-coded status (hot/cold/steady) shows if a manager is on form or slumping. Drives urgency — "Am I trending up or down?"

## 2. Rank Movement Tracker
Per-gameweek rank change with up/down arrows and magnitude. Also shows cumulative rank gained and lost across the season. Managers obsessively check if they're climbing or falling.

## 3. Best & Worst Gameweek
Single best and single worst gameweek with exact points, gameweek number, and rank. Everyone remembers their best week and cringes at their worst — makes them want to revisit.

## 4. Captain Hall of Fame / Shame
Which players were captained most and their average return. "You captained Haaland 12 times for an avg of 8.3 pts." Also a "should have captained" counter — total points left on the table from bad captain picks.

## 5. Bench Points Waste
Total points left on the bench across the season. Devastating stat — managers can't stop checking how many points they wasted. Also per-gameweek breakdown and worst bench week.

## 6. Consistency Rating
A score (0-100) and letter grade (A-F) based on standard deviation of points. High consistency = reliable performer, low = explosive but volatile. Managers compare this against league rivals.

## 7. Player Loyalty Index
Which players have been in the squad the longest (most gameweeks owned). Creates attachment — "I've owned Saka for 20 weeks." Shows total points contributed while owned.

## 8. Position Breakdown
Points contribution by position (GK/DEF/MID/FWD). A donut chart showing where their points come from. "Are you a defense-heavy team or do you live and die by midfield?"

## 9. Hit Merchant / Transfer Addict Score
Number of gameweeks where hits were taken vs free transfers. "You took a hit in 18 out of 29 gameweeks." Also shows total hit cost — were they worth it?

## 10. Chip Timing Grade
For each chip used, compare the points that gameweek to the manager's season average. Grade A-F on timing. "Your Triple Captain in GW12: A+ (42 pts vs 58 avg)." Creates debate and regret.

## 11. Green Arrows / Red Arrows Count
How many gameweeks the rank went up vs down. Total green arrows vs red arrows. Managers are addicted to seeing green — this stat gamifies the rank movement.

## 12. Squad Value Tracker
Net squad value change over the season with an area chart. Shows value evolution across gameweeks. Managers chase team value — this validates or punishes that strategy.

## 13. Head-to-Head Rival Comparison
Side-by-side comparison of two managers in the same league across all gameweeks. Who won each week? Win/loss/draw record. Makes league play competitive and personal.

## 14. Season Timeline / Story
A narrative timeline of the season auto-generated from data: "Peaked in GW12 with 95 points" or "Hot streak from GW8 — 5 weeks above average." People love reading their own story.

## 15. Differential Finder
Latest gameweek squad breakdown showing points per player. "You own Palmer (not in 8 of 12 league teams) — that's your edge." Drives engagement before every deadline.

## 16. Expected Points vs Actual (Bench Regret)
For each gameweek, show what the total WOULD have been with optimal bench ordering. "You left 12 points on the bench in GW23." Painful but addictive.

## 17. Lucky / Unlucky Index
Compare captain points to the highest-scoring player that gameweek. If the captain was the top scorer = lucky/smart. Aggregate lucky vs unlucky gameweeks across the season.

## 18. League Position History Chart
Line chart of league rank over gameweeks. Managers check this obsessively. Shows the full journey — early struggles, mid-season surges, final-day drama.

## 19. Manager Comparison Percentile
"Your 1,847 points put you in the top 12% of all managers on this platform." Benchmark against all managers in the system, not just their league. Creates a global competitive feeling.

## 20. Gameweek Predictability Score
How template vs differential a manager's squad is based on ownership percentages. Drives pre-deadline engagement — "Should I go template or risk a differential?"
